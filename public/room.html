[ guessed appearance ]

  1. An excerpt from a random Wikipedia article will appear on the big screen
  2. Using your smartphone, you must submit the title of an article you think the excerpt could have come from
  3. When everyone's finished, the correct answer - along with all with your answers, will appear on the screen
  4. You must then use your smartphone to vote for whichever answer you think is the truth
  5. You get points for guessing the answer correctly, but also when players vote for your answer
  6. So try to make your answer obscure - but keep it believable, or no one will vote for you!

[ excerpt opinions ]

  1. You will recieve the title of an article chosen at random from Wikipedia on your smartphone
  2. Using your smartphone, you must submit a snippet of text that could have come from the Wikipedia article with that title
  3. e.g., for the "Nazi chic" article, you might submit: "To keep morale up, the Nazis held a fashion show once a month"
  4. When everyone's finished, each article-excerpt pair will appear on the big screen one after another
  5. You must then use your smartphone to vote for whoever's snippet you find the funniest, most clever, or by whatever metric you deem worthy of your vote!
  6. Everyone answers twice this round! So make sure you submit a snippet for both of your articles

[ you complete me ]

  1. An incomplete excerpt from a random Wikipedia article will appear on your smartphone
  2. Using your smartphone, you must complete the excerpt where you see the blank
  3. e.g., for an excerpt saying "Nikola Tesla was known for inventing ____", you might submit "two for tuesday's at Domino's pizza®"
  4. When everyone's finished, each excerpt pair will appear on the big screen one after another
  5. You must then use your smartphone to vote for whoever's snippet you find the funniest, most clever, or by whatever metric you deem worthy of your vote!
  6. Everyone answers twice this round! So make sure you fill in a blank for both snippets
